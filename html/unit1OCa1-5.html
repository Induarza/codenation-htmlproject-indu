1.5 Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software – how do you select the right kind of technology for the job?

Choosing software is not a quick or easy process. Many small business owners and managers know they need a new system but are put off by the number of competing products, the amount of time needed to evaluate each one, or a fear of choosing the wrong solution. We know you can’t afford to make a mistake, in small businesses we just don’t have resources to spare, so it’s important to get it right first time. Most of the small business give there Software systems outsourcing.

1. Know your objective

  • Know the process. What must the system do?
  • Know the pain points. What problems must the new solution solve?
  • Know it’s value. What would a solution be worth?

    • 2. Create a list of software requirements

  • Functional requirements
  • Usability requirements.
  • Technological preferences.
  • Budgetary requirements.
  • Reporting requirements
  • Scalability

    • 3. Start searching for relevant applications

  • It’s best to put them into a spreadsheet, ready for comparing later. At this point though, don’t waste time evaluating the systems, just list them all out.

    • 4. Start excluding solutions

  • Technology preferences. If it only works on Macs and you are on Windows, great, that’s one less to look at. If it is on-premises, and you’re looking for a cloud-based solution – cross it off the list.
  • Budget. If it is too expensive, make a note of the price and move on.

    • 5. Start trialling your selected software:

  • Learnability
  • Efficiency
  • Errors
  • Satisfaction
