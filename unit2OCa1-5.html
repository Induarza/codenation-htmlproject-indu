1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

  • Copyright is the right of someone who as originally created and the person who created it owns the rights to that image. That is refered to as copyright only the owner has the right to produce or publish that work, display the work in public and use that copies to sell or donate.
  • All the companies have trademarks and they are protected by the law.
  • The only exception to copyright is if you use an image from public domains such as Google Images and college or school or course work. Includes images,photographs, texts etc

    • Therefore, we risk infringing copyright if we reproduce someone else's website content on our website without permission or license. Hypertext links in a website are exceptions, these link one website to another website. It is not clear whether linking from one website to another website infringes copyright in the linked website. It is possible that anyone whose website includes links could be held responsible for any page to which it is linked, even if this link is indirect and involves passing through unconnected parties. Ideally, we should ensure that, if we wish to include a link to another website, we have permission from the owner of the website such as a linking agreement. in most cases, if the website is not commercial and for educational purposes
